# 📚 **Documentation: Physical Processes (0–10 MeV)**

### 🟢 **0 – Transportation / UserStepMax**
- *Transportation*: The movement of particles through geometry volumes without interactions.
- *UserStepMax*: Artificial limit to particle step length, defined by user constraints (not physical).

---

### 🔵 **1 – Photoelectric Effect (phot)**
Interaction of a gamma photon with an atom, where the photon is completely absorbed, and an electron (photoelectron) is emitted from an inner atomic shell. Dominant at lower gamma energies (<~100 keV).

---

### 🔵 **2 – Compton Scattering (compt)**
Inelastic scattering of a photon on a loosely bound electron. The photon transfers part of its energy to the electron (Compton electron) and continues with reduced energy. Important at intermediate energies (100 keV–5 MeV).

---

### 🔵 **3 – Pair Production (conv)**
Creation of an electron-positron pair when a high-energy photon (>1.022 MeV) interacts with the electromagnetic field of an atomic nucleus. Essential for high-energy gamma interactions (>~1 MeV).

---

### 🔵 **4 – Rayleigh Scattering (Rayl)**
Elastic scattering of photons on atoms without energy loss (photon maintains its energy). Relevant mainly at very low photon energies.

---

### 🔵 **5 – Electron Ionization (eIoni)**
Energy loss of electrons through ionization and excitation of atoms along their path, resulting in the ejection of secondary electrons.

---

### 🔵 **6 – Multiple Coulomb Scattering (msc)**
Multiple elastic scattering events of charged particles (electrons, protons, etc.) with atomic nuclei, changing particle direction and position. Crucial for particle beam broadening in media.

---

### 🔵 **7 – Electron-Positron Annihilation (annihil)**
Interaction between a positron and electron, resulting in annihilation and emission of two (or rarely three) gamma photons, typically of 511 keV each.

---

### 🔵 **8 – Bremsstrahlung (eBrem)**
Radiation emitted when electrons decelerate or deflect under the Coulomb field of atomic nuclei, resulting in continuous X-ray or gamma photon spectra.

---

### 🔵 **9 – Radioactive Decay**
Spontaneous transformation of unstable atomic nuclei into more stable ones, emitting radiation such as alpha, beta particles, gamma photons, and neutrinos.

---

### 🔵 **10 – Hadronic Elastic Scattering (hadElastic)**
Elastic scattering of neutrons or other hadrons on atomic nuclei, without nuclear excitation or breakup. Important for neutron moderation.

---

### 🔵 **11 – Neutron Inelastic Interaction (neutronInelastic)**
Inelastic scattering of neutrons causing excitation and breakup of nuclei, producing secondary gamma photons, neutrons, or charged fragments.

---

### 🔵 **12 – Neutron Capture (nCapture)**
Process in which a neutron is absorbed by a nucleus, resulting in a heavier isotope often accompanied by gamma emission.

---

### 🔵 **13 – Proton Inelastic Interaction (protonInelastic)**
Inelastic scattering of protons on nuclei, leading to nuclear excitation or breakup, accompanied by emission of secondary particles (gammas, neutrons, protons).

---

### 🔵 **14 – Alpha Inelastic Interaction (alphaInelastic)**
Inelastic interaction of alpha particles (helium nuclei) with atomic nuclei, typically causing nuclear excitation, secondary particle emission, and gamma radiation.

---

### 🔵 **15 – Ion Ionization (ionIoni)**
Energy loss by heavy charged ions (protons, alpha, heavier ions) through ionization of atoms, producing secondary electrons along the ion's path.

---

### 🔵 **16 – Auger Electrons (Auger)**
Electrons emitted from atoms during relaxation processes, following an inner-shell ionization event (e.g., after photoelectric effect or electron capture).

---

### 🔵 **17 – X-ray Fluorescence (phot_fluo)**
Emission of characteristic X-rays when an atom returns to lower energy states after inner-shell electron vacancies, typically created by photoelectric absorption or ionization.

---

### 🔵 **18 – PIXE (Proton-Induced X-ray Emission)**
Characteristic X-ray emission resulting specifically from inner-shell ionization induced by proton bombardment.

---

### 🔵 **19 – Muon Ionization (muIoni)**
Ionization energy loss by muons passing through matter, producing secondary electrons.

---

### 🔵 **20 – Muon Bremsstrahlung (muBrems)**
Bremsstrahlung (braking radiation) emitted by muons decelerating in the nuclear Coulomb fields of atoms.

---

### 🔵 **21 – Muon Pair Production (muPairProd)**
Creation of electron-positron pairs induced by energetic muons interacting with atomic nuclei.

---

### 🔵 **22 – Cherenkov Radiation (Cerenkov)**
Electromagnetic radiation emitted when charged particles travel through a medium faster than the phase velocity of light in that medium, resulting in a characteristic optical emission.

---

### 🔵 **23 – Scintillation**
Emission of visible or ultraviolet light photons by a material when it is excited by ionizing radiation, widely used in radiation detection and imaging.

---

### 🔵 **24 – Synchrotron Radiation**
Electromagnetic radiation emitted when relativistic charged particles (usually electrons) are accelerated radially (change direction) in magnetic fields.

---

### ⚠️ **99 – Other (Unknown)**
Reserved for unclassified or unexpected interactions occurring outside standard definitions. Useful for debugging or future expansions.

---

### 🔶 **Classification of Secondary Electrons:**

- **0 – Primary electron (initial beam electron)**
- **1 – Photoelectron (electron emitted by photoelectric effect)**
- **2 – Compton electron (electron emitted by Compton scattering)**
- **3 – Electron from pair production (pair-produced electron)**
- **4 – Auger electron**
- **5 – Electron from X-ray fluorescence**
- **6 – Electron produced by electron-ionization**
- **7 – Electron produced by ion-ionization**
- **8 – Beta electron (from radioactive decay)**
- **99 – Other secondary electrons (unknown origin)**
